Jammu & Kashmir

Jammu and Kashmir attracts tourists for its diverse landscape, cultures and weather as well as for adventure activities in the Himalayas such as skiing, mountaineering, hiking, trekking and camping. Some major tourist attractions in Jammu and Kashmir are Katra, Patnitop, Buddha Amarnath and Mughal Gardens, Gulmarg, Pahalgam, Bhaderwah, Devigol Bunjwah, Dal Lake, Sonamarg and Paddar. Every year, thousands of Hindu pilgrims visit holy shrines of Vaishno Devi and Amarnath which has had a significant impact on the state's economy

Jammu Division is one of the top tourist destinations of India.[2] Gulmarg, one of the most popular ski resort destinations in India, is also home to the world's highest green golf course.[3] Tourism has declined in the past thirty years.
